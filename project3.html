Narrative-Driven Experience

Level & Narrative Designer

Project Overview

Designed a story-driven campaign with 8 interconnected levels where the environment and level progression reinforce narrative themes. Collaborated closely with writers to ensure that gameplay, level design, and story elements worked together cohesively to create an emotionally resonant experience.

Duration

14 months

Team Size

15 people

Role

Level & Narrative Designer

Tools

Unreal Engine 5

Key Responsibilities

  • Designed 8 main campaign levels with narrative beats integrated into gameplay
  • Collaborated with narrative team on story structure and pacing
  • Created visual narrative elements through environmental design and level progression
  • Designed character interactions and story moments tied to level progression
  • Implemented cutscenes and camera work to enhance emotional beats

Narrative Integration

Environmental Storytelling

Levels communicate story through visual design, object placement, and environmental details rather than relying solely on dialogue.

Gameplay as Narrative

Mechanical challenges and level progression reinforce character arcs and thematic elements of the story.

Emotional Pacing

Carefully balanced gameplay intensity with character development and story moments for emotional impact.

World Building

Each level expands the game's world and backstory through careful environmental design and detail.

Campaign Structure

Act I: Origins (Levels 1-2)

Introduction to the protagonist and their world. Levels establish visual style, core mechanics, and initial story hook.

Act II: Exploration (Levels 3-5)

Deepening the narrative as the protagonist uncovers mysteries. Levels become more complex, introducing new mechanics and themes.

Act III: Resolution (Levels 6-8)

Climactic levels where story and gameplay converge. Challenges directly relate to character motivations and thematic resolution.

Collaboration & Process

  • Early collaboration between writers and designers ensures cohesive storytelling
  • Visual language of levels can communicate narrative more effectively than exposition
  • Character emotions should be reflected in level design and pacing
  • Story moments have more impact when integrated into gameplay rather than interrupting it
  • Environmental details reward observant players and deepen their engagement with the narrative
