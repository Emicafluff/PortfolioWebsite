Environmental Puzzle System

Game Designer

Project Overview

Created an innovative environmental puzzle mechanic system that allows players to manipulate and interact with the game world to overcome challenges and progress through levels. Designed 15+ unique puzzles with varying difficulty levels, elegant solutions, and replayability through multiple solution paths.

Duration

12 months

Team Size

8 people

Role

Game Designer

Tools

Unity Engine

Key Responsibilities

  • Designed the core environmental interaction mechanics and systems
  • Created 15+ puzzles with documented solutions and difficulty ratings
  • Prototyped puzzle concepts and iterated based on player feedback
  • Worked with programmers to implement mechanics efficiently
  • Playtested extensively to identify difficulty spikes and unclear solutions

Design Highlights

Elegant Mechanics

Core mechanics are simple to learn but allow for complex puzzle designs and emergent solutions.

Progressive Difficulty

Puzzles introduce new mechanics and challenge types gradually, building player understanding throughout the experience.

Multiple Solutions

Many puzzles have multiple valid solutions, rewarding creative problem-solving and exploration.

Visual Clarity

Environmental design clearly communicates which objects are interactive and what interactions are possible.

Puzzle Categories

Mechanical Puzzles (5 puzzles)

Players manipulate physical objects in the environment to create pathways, access hidden areas, or trigger mechanisms.

Environmental Interaction Puzzles (5 puzzles)

Players interact with environmental elements like water, gravity fields, or lighting to solve complex challenges.

Combination Puzzles (5 puzzles)

Advanced puzzles requiring players to combine multiple mechanics and environmental interactions to progress.

Playtesting Insights

  • Clear visual feedback is essential for players to understand puzzle mechanics
  • Hint systems should be integrated naturally into the game world
  • Players prefer puzzles with multiple valid solutions to single "correct" solutions
  • Difficulty progression matters more than absolute puzzle difficulty
